The Challenge: Rising Heat

New Delhi faces a critical Urban Heat Island (UHI) effect. Our time-series analysis (2013–2023) reveals a consistent rise in Land Surface Temperature (LST), correlated directly with the expansion of impermeable concrete surfaces (NDBI > 0.05).

Without intervention, 2050 projections estimate a further +1.5°C to +2.0°C increase in core urban zones, reducing livability scores to "Unsuitable" levels.

Critical Finding

Areas with NDBI > 0.05 and NDVI < 0.25 (dense concrete/low veg) are effectively "heat traps," consistently measuring 3-5°C higher than surrounding green zones.

The Solution:
Targeted Revegetation

Using a Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis (MCDA) engine, we identified specific zones for intervention. The algorithm targets pixels that meet two strict criteria:

  • High Heat Stress: Areas with NDBI > 0.05 (High urban density).
  • Low Green Cover: Areas with NDVI < 0.25 (Sparse vegetation).

The Scenario: By converting these specific "Heat Trap" zones into urban micro-forests (increasing NDVI by +0.25), our model predicts a localized temperature drop of 1.5°C and a significant boost in Livability Scores.

Micro-Climate Cooling

By strategically planting in the identified 14.2% "Moderate" transition zones, we effectively break the continuous thermal mass of the city, creating cooling corridors.

Data-Driven Policy

This Digital Twin enables the Delhi NCR Planning Board to move from "blanket plantation" drives to precision afforestation, allocating budget exactly where the thermal ROI is highest.
